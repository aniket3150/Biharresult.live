Step-by-Step Bihar Post-Matric Scholarship Apply Guide

Bihar Post-Matric Scholarship is a major support scheme for students pursuing higher studies after Class 10. Many applications are delayed or rejected due to avoidable mistakes. This guide explains a practical workflow that helps students complete the process correctly from registration to final verification.

1. Understand Eligibility Before Registration

Do not begin form filling without checking category, income criteria, course type, and institution eligibility. The most common issue is applying under wrong category or using ineligible institution details. Match your caste certificate, admission details, and income certificate date with current notification rules.

2. Keep Documents Ready in Correct Format

  • Aadhaar card, mobile number linked for OTP verification.
  • Caste certificate and income certificate (valid and readable).
  • Current admission receipt or fee structure proof.
  • Bank passbook with student name matching scholarship profile.
  • Recent passport photo and signature as per portal size rules.

Before upload, verify that file names are simple, file size is within limits, and all scanned documents are clear. Blurry uploads often cause manual recheck delays.

3. Registration Process Best Practice

Use a stable network and complete registration in one session. Save your registration number immediately in phone notes and email. Use an active email address and phone number because OTP and status updates may come there later.

4. Fill Academic and Personal Details Carefully

Your name, father/mother name, date of birth, and Aadhaar details must match official documents exactly. Avoid short forms, spelling variations, and inconsistent initials. For academic details, enter board/university, pass year, roll code, and marks exactly as mark sheet records.

5. Bank and Payment Readiness

Scholarship disbursal depends on proper bank mapping. Confirm that account is active, IFSC is correct, and account holder name matches student profile. Avoid using closed/inactive accounts or accounts with unresolved KYC issues.

6. Preview and Final Submission Checklist

  • All mandatory fields completed.
  • No mismatch in personal and document data.
  • All uploads visible in preview.
  • Bank details rechecked twice.
  • Application PDF downloaded after final submit.

7. Post-Submission Tracking

After submission, check status periodically: pending at institute, district-level verification, or approved for payment. If correction window opens, respond immediately. Keep all original documents ready for physical verification if requested by institute authority.

Common Rejection Reasons

  • Name mismatch between Aadhaar and application.
  • Invalid income/caste certificate or expired certificate.
  • Wrong course year and incorrect admission details.
  • Unreadable uploads and wrong file formats.
  • Duplicate registration by same candidate.

Final advice: treat scholarship form as an official legal document. Fill slowly, verify each section, and keep copies of final submission and uploaded files.
